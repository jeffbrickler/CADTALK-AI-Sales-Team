# Objection Playbook

Use this pattern: label, clarify, re-anchor, confirm path.

## Price

Label: `It sounds like the investment is higher than expected.`

Clarify: `Is the concern total cost, timing of budget, or confidence in the return?`

Re-anchor: `The cost only makes sense if it removes [manual effort/risk/delay] and protects [business outcome].`

Confirm: `If we can connect the investment to that outcome clearly, is price still the blocker?`

## Timing

Label: `It sounds like timing may be the bigger issue than fit.`

Clarify: `What is driving the timing concern? Team bandwidth, budget cycle, ERP timeline, or competing priorities?`

Re-anchor: `Waiting may be reasonable, but we should make the cost of waiting explicit.`

Confirm: `What date would make this realistic, and what would need to happen before then?`

## Implementation Risk

Label: `It sounds like the concern is not whether the solution can work, but whether the implementation will land cleanly.`

Clarify: `Is the risk ownership, data quality, IT access, testing bandwidth, or change management?`

Re-anchor: `That is exactly why the implementation plan includes [risk controls].`

Confirm: `If those controls are in place, does implementation risk still prevent approval?`

## Missing Stakeholder

Label: `It sounds like there is another stakeholder who needs to be comfortable before this can move forward.`

Clarify: `Who is that person, and what will they care about most?`

Re-anchor: `We should not ask them to review a generic proposal. We should give them the decision case tied to their concern.`

Confirm: `Should we schedule a focused approval call with them?`

## Budget Process

Label: `It sounds like the business case may be there, but the budget path is not clear yet.`

Clarify: `Is there an existing budget source, or does this require new approval?`

Re-anchor: `Then the next step is not more product discussion. It is building the approval case.`

Confirm: `Who owns that approval, and what format do they need?`
