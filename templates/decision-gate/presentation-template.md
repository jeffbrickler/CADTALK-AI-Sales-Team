# Executive Presentation Template

Create 8-10 slides. Keep each slide decision-oriented.

## Slide 1: Decision Gate Objective

- Purpose: establish this is a move-forward decision meeting.
- Core message: the demo and architecture are complete; today is about business case, investment, risk, and approval path.
- What to show: meeting objective, decision required, agenda.
- Talk track: `The goal today is to decide whether this proposal is strong enough to move into approval, and if not, identify the exact gap.`
- Buyer question: `Does that match what you want to accomplish today?`

## Slide 2: Confirmed Business Problem

- Purpose: prove the rep understands the buyer's operating pain.
- Core message: current state creates measurable risk, cost, delay, or manual work.
- What to show: 3-5 bullets from discovery using buyer language.
- Talk track: `Here is what we heard. I want to validate this before we discuss cost.`
- Buyer question: `Where is this incomplete or overstated?`

## Slide 3: Cost of Inaction

- Purpose: make delay concrete.
- Core message: doing nothing has a cost.
- What to show: time lost, error risk, manual effort, missed deadline, ERP/operational impact.
- Talk track: `The decision is not software versus no cost. It is investment versus continued operational drag.`
- Buyer question: `What happens if this waits another quarter?`

## Slide 4: Agreed Solution and Scope

- Purpose: confirm what is included and what is not.
- Core message: the proposal maps to the agreed business outcome.
- What to show: solution components, scope boundaries, systems involved, user groups.
- Talk track: `This is the scope we recommend based on the architecture review.`
- Buyer question: `Is anything critical missing from this scope?`

## Slide 5: Implementation Path to Value

- Purpose: reduce fear of failed deployment.
- Core message: value requires a controlled implementation path, not just software access.
- What to show: phases, timeline, milestones, acceptance points.
- Talk track: `The implementation plan is what protects the business case.`
- Buyer question: `Does this implementation path match your team's capacity?`

## Slide 6: Customer Responsibilities and Readiness

- Purpose: create shared ownership.
- Core message: the customer has specific responsibilities that determine speed and success.
- What to show: project owner, system access, data readiness, testing participation, decision cadence.
- Talk track: `The biggest controllable risk is readiness: ownership, access, data, and timely decisions.`
- Buyer question: `Who owns these items on your side?`

## Slide 7: Investment Summary

- Purpose: present cost after value and risk are clear.
- Core message: the investment funds the outcome, implementation, and operating capability.
- What to show: subscription, implementation/services, term, optional add-ons, payment terms.
- Talk track: `The proposed investment is [amount]. This includes [scope]. The reason this is structured this way is [business rationale].`
- Buyer question: pause first. Then ask: `What part of the investment needs more context?`

## Slide 8: Decision Options

- Purpose: frame the buyer's choices.
- Core message: there are only a few rational paths.
- What to show: approve now, approve after listed open items, defer with known consequence, or stop.
- Talk track: `I do not want to create fake momentum. Based on what we reviewed, I see these decision paths.`
- Buyer question: `Which path is most accurate?`

## Slide 9: Mutual Action Plan

- Purpose: convert interest into execution.
- Core message: every next step needs owner and date.
- What to show: action, owner, due date, dependency.
- Talk track: `If we are moving forward, these are the steps to get from today to signature and kickoff.`
- Buyer question: `Who owns each step?`

## Slide 10: Approval Ask

- Purpose: close.
- Core message: ask directly for the next commitment.
- What to show: recommended decision and date.
- Talk track: `Based on the business case, fit, and implementation plan, are you comfortable moving this into approval?`
- Buyer question: `If not, what specific gap do we need to close?`
