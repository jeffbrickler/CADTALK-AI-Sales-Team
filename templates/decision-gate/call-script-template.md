# Call Script Template

## Opening

`Thanks for making the time. Since the demo and solution architecture have already been reviewed, I do not want to use today as another product walkthrough. The goal is to decide whether this proposal is strong enough to move forward, and if not, identify the exact gap.`

`I suggest we cover four things: the business case, the proposed scope, the implementation path, and the investment. Then we decide the next step. Is that the right use of the meeting?`

## Reset the Business Case

`Before we discuss cost, I want to make sure we are aligned on the problem. What we heard is [problem], which is creating [impact]. The outcome you want is [outcome]. Where is that incomplete or wrong?`

## Confirm Success Criteria

`For this to be successful, we believe the solution needs to achieve [criteria]. If those are achieved, would this solve the business problem you brought us in to address?`

## Transition to Recommendation

`Based on the demo, architecture review, and your requirements, our recommendation is [solution/package]. The reason we are recommending this option is [business reason], not because it is the most software we can sell.`

## Implementation Risk Control

`The largest risk in projects like this is rarely the presentation layer. It is ownership, data readiness, access, and testing cadence. That is why the implementation plan matters as much as the license.`

`Here is what we will own: [vendor responsibilities]. Here is what your team will need to own: [customer responsibilities].`

## Present Investment

`The investment for this is [price]. That includes [subscription/scope] and [implementation/services].`

Pause. Do not keep talking.

If silence continues:

`What part of that investment needs more context?`

## Surface the Real Objection

`What would prevent this from being approved?`

`Is the concern value, timing, budget process, or implementation risk?`

`If we solved that concern, would you be comfortable moving this forward?`

## Close Path

If approved:

`Great. Who owns signature, and what date should we work toward?`

If procurement/legal:

`Who owns procurement, what do they need from us, and when can we get that started?`

If more stakeholders:

`Who needs to be included, what do they care about, and should we run a short executive alignment meeting with them?`

If unclear:

`I may be reading this wrong, but it sounds like we do not yet have a decision process. What is the cleanest path from today's discussion to yes or no?`

If no decision:

`That is fair. What is the specific reason this is not ready to move forward?`
