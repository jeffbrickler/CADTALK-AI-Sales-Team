# SaaS Sales Enablement Engine (CADTALK)

The enablement engine `/ct-train` runs in **Enablement Mode** (and `/ct-se` draws
on Sections 4–5 for discovery/demo structure). Build the tools, training, playbooks,
and resources that let the CADTALK sales team execute at a high level. Sourced from
Aaron Ross's *Predictable Revenue* and Rob Walling's *The SaaS Playbook*.

**Where the CADTALK specifics live:** pull ICP from `/ct-icp`, qualification from
`/ct-qualify`, objections from `/ct-objections`, competitive intel from
`/ct-competitors`, pricing from `/ct-qualify` Step 7, and voice from
`references/cadtalk-voice-reference.md`. This engine is the *structure*; those skills
and references are the *content*. Don't reinvent CADTALK facts here — pull them.

You are building the systems that allow a sales team to execute at a consistently high level — whether they are in month one or year three. Sales enablement is the infrastructure that makes the sales process teachable, repeatable, and improvable. Without it, you are completely dependent on individual talent. With it, even average hires can produce above-average results.

Run every section relevant to the request. Always start with Section 1 to establish context.

## Section 1: Establish Context

Collect these inputs before producing any enablement material. Ask for anything missing. For CADTALK, most of this is already known — pull it rather than asking.

1. **Product:** What does the product do, in one sentence a non-technical buyer would use? (CADTALK: CAD/PLM-to-ERP integration that eliminates manual BOM re-entry.)
2. **ACV and sales cycle:** How much does it cost per year and how long does a typical deal take? (See `/ct-qualify` Step 7 price matrix.)
3. **Buyer persona:** Who is the decision maker? Who is the champion? What do they care about? (VP/Dir Engineering, IT Director; economic buyer varies by motion.)
4. **Team composition:** Who needs to be enabled? SDRs, AEs, CSMs, or all three?
5. **Primary request:** Is this a new hire onboarding, a specific tool (objection guide, battlecard, email templates), a training format, or a full enablement audit?
6. **Gaps:** What is the current team struggling with? Where do deals stall? What do reps get wrong most often?

## Section 2: The Sales Playbook Structure

A sales playbook is the single document every rep can open and know exactly what to do at every stage. It is not a slide deck. It is not a wiki. It is a step-by-step operational guide.

**A complete sales playbook contains:**

### Module 1: Company and Product Foundation
- What the company does and why it exists (2–3 sentences, buyer language, no jargon)
- The specific problem the product solves (not what it does — what pain it eliminates)
- The outcome the customer achieves (what does their world look like 90 days after buying?)
- What makes it different from the two closest competitors (one sentence each, factual not marketing — pull from `/ct-competitors`)

### Module 2: The Ideal Customer Profile
- Firmographic criteria: industry, size, geography
- Technographic signals: tools they use that indicate readiness to buy
- Trigger events: situations that create urgency right now
- Who to reach: decision maker title, champion title, economic buyer title
- Disqualification criteria: who is explicitly NOT a fit and why
- **Pull the live CADTALK ICP from `/ct-icp` — don't restate it here.**

### Module 3: Prospecting Guide
- Target account criteria and how to build a list
- Referral email templates (Cold Calling 2.0 format — see Section 3)
- Sequence structure: how many touches, over how many days, through which channels
- How to handle non-responses, referrals, and "send me more information"
- Account status stages and definitions
- **CADTALK outreach copy is generated by `/ct-outreach` and voiced via `/ct-voice`.**

### Module 4: Qualification Framework
- The qualification criteria (BANTED: Budget, Authority, Need, Timeline, Evaluation, Decision Process)
- Scoping call structure (question sequence)
- Go/no-go decision criteria after the first call
- How to disqualify gracefully without burning the relationship
- **CADTALK runs WGLL + BANT via `/ct-qualify`; the commit gate via `/ct-commit`; discovery scoring via `/ct-score`.**

### Module 5: Discovery and Demo Guide
- Discovery call structure (what questions to ask and in what order — see Section 4)
- How to connect features to the specific problems uncovered in discovery
- Demo script outline: opening, problem framing, solution proof, success plan, next step
- What NOT to demo (features that confuse or distract)
- Common demo mistakes and how to avoid them
- **CADTALK technical demo prep (CAD×ERP fit, Brain-grounded script) is `/ct-se`; call prep is `/ct-prep`.**

### Module 6: Objection Handling
- See Section 5 of this reference for the framework. **CADTALK's live objection library is `/ct-objections`.**

### Module 7: Competitive Intelligence
- Top competitors: how they position, where they are strong, where they are weak
- How to handle: "We're already using [Competitor]"
- How to handle: "You're more expensive than [Competitor]"
- What to avoid saying about competitors (never trash-talk; address factually)
- **CADTALK battlecards come from `/ct-competitors`; template in Section 6.**

### Module 8: Closing and Negotiation
- The Success Plan step (before any negotiation — see `references/deal-coach.md` Section 5)
- How to advance a deal that has gone dark (see `references/deal-coach.md` Section 7)
- What to do when a prospect says "we need a discount"
- How to handle "we need to think about it"
- Standard deal terms and approval authority

### Module 9: Post-Close Handoff
- What the AE gives the CSM at handoff
- Success Plan documentation
- Customer kickoff call protocol
- **CADTALK order handoff to fulfillment is `/ct-fulfill` (one SOP-shaped email per won deal).**

## Section 3: Prospecting Templates (Cold Calling 2.0 Format)

These templates follow the Cold Calling 2.0 referral email format. They must be customized per company and ICP before use. Do not use generic language. **For CADTALK, generate live copy through `/ct-outreach` and voice-check via `/ct-voice` — these are structure only.**

**Template Type 1: Executive Referral Email**
*Goal: Get a referral to the right person from someone one or two levels above them.*

```
Subject: [Their Company] + CADTALK

[First Name],

I'm reaching out because we've been helping [type of company]
[specific outcome in their language]. I wanted to see if you
could point me to the right person on your team to explore
whether this might be relevant for [Their Company].

Any help would be appreciated — even a simple "not the right
time" is useful.

[Your Name]
```

**Rules:**
- Under 75 words total
- Plain text. No HTML, no logos.
- One question only
- No product pitch. No feature list. No case study.
- No "I hope this finds you well"

**Template Type 2: Triggered Outreach**
*For prospects where a trigger event (new hire, funding round, ERP migration) has been identified.*

```
Subject: Noticed [trigger event] at [Their Company]

[First Name],

I saw that [Their Company] recently [trigger event — e.g.,
"announced a Series B" or "hired a new VP of Operations"].

Companies going through [situation] often find that [specific
problem your product solves] becomes a priority. Is this
something worth a 15-minute conversation to see if there's
any fit?

[Your Name]
```

**Template Type 3: Follow-Up After No Response (Day 5 and Day 10)**

```
Subject: Re: [Original Subject]

[First Name], bubbling this back up in case it got buried.

Is this worth a quick conversation, or is the timing just
not right?

[Your Name]
```

**Sequence Cadence:**
- Day 1: Referral email (Template 1 or 2)
- Day 5: Follow-up (Template 3)
- Day 10: Final follow-up with slightly different angle
- Day 15: "Breakup" email — acknowledge it may not be relevant, leave door open
- Day 30+: Move to "Future" status. Re-engage in 90–180 days with fresh context.

## Section 4: The Discovery Call Playbook

Discovery is the most important call in the sales process. Everything else — the demo, the proposal, the close — depends on how well you understood their world. A bad discovery means a bad close rate. **`/ct-prep` builds the per-call brief; `/ct-score` grades the discovery afterward. This is the underlying structure both rest on.**

### Pre-Call Preparation (Required Before Every First Call)
- Research the company: size, industry, recent news, tech stack (`/ct-research`)
- Research the contact: LinkedIn, recent posts, role, tenure (`/ct-contacts`)
- Know the ICP trigger event that brought them in or prompted outreach
- Prepare two or three tailored questions based on their specific situation

### Discovery Call Structure

**Opening (2 min):**
"Before I show you anything, can I ask a few questions to make sure this conversation is actually relevant for your situation?"

This sets the tone as a research call, not a pitch.

**Org mapping (5 min):**
"Can you walk me through how [relevant function] is structured at [Company]?"
Goal: Understand who does what, who has authority, and how decisions are made.

**Current state (5–7 min):**
"How are you handling [the problem space] today?"
"What tools or processes do you have in place for this?"
Goal: Understand their status quo before presenting anything.

**Pain and cost (5–7 min):**
"What's not working about how you're doing it today?"
"What does that cost you?" (time, money, risk, opportunity)
"If this keeps going the way it is, what happens?"
Goal: Quantify the pain. A problem with a number attached closes faster. (For CADTALK: engineer count × BOM-entry hours × loaded rate — see `/ct-qualify` Step 6.)

**Success picture (3–5 min):**
"If you solved this perfectly, what would that look like in 90 days?"
"What would have to change for you to feel like this was worth the investment?"
Goal: Get them to articulate their own success criteria. This becomes the Success Plan.

**Decision process (5 min):**
"If you decided to move forward with something, how would that decision typically get made?"
"Who else would be involved?"
"What does the evaluation process look like?"
Goal: Map the buying committee and understand the approval chain before investing more time.

**Timeline (2 min):**
"Is there a date by which this needs to be solved?"
"What is driving that timeline?"
Goal: Identify forcing functions. No forcing function = long sales cycle.

**Next step (3 min):**
Propose a specific next step based on what you learned.
Do not say "I'll send you some information." Define who does what, by when.
Examples: "Can we schedule a 45-minute demo on Thursday? I'll show you exactly how [specific thing they care about] works." or "Based on what you told me, I'd like to introduce you to our [specific resource] — can we get 30 minutes next week?"

## Section 5: Objection Handling Guide

Every objection has a root cause. Handling an objection without addressing the root cause just moves it to the next call. **CADTALK's live, product-specific objection responses are `/ct-objections`. This is the framework underneath it.**

### The Four-Step Framework for Every Objection

1. **Acknowledge:** Show you heard them. Do not immediately counter.
2. **Clarify:** Is this the real objection or a surface-level deflection?
3. **Respond:** Address the root cause, not the stated objection.
4. **Confirm:** Did that answer their concern, or is there more?

### Common Objections and Responses

**"We don't have budget."**
- Clarify: "Is there genuinely no budget allocated for this area, or is this not a current priority?"
- If no budget: "When does your budget cycle reset? Can we stay in touch and revisit in [timeframe]?"
- If not a priority: "Help me understand — you mentioned [their pain]. What is the cost of leaving that unsolved for another 12 months?"

**"We need to think about it."**
- Clarify: "What specifically is driving the need to think about it?"
- Usually means: missing information, unresolved concern, or internal approval needed
- Respond: "That's completely fair. To help make that decision easier, what information would be most useful?"
- Follow up: Ask when they'll have had the chance to think about it, and schedule the follow-up call before getting off the current call.

**"We're already using [Competitor]."**
- Acknowledge: "That makes sense — [Competitor] is a solid option for [what they're good at]."
- Clarify: "What do you like most about how it's working for you today? And is there anything you wish worked differently?"
- Goal: If there is no dissatisfaction, disqualify and move on. If there is, focus on the gap.

**"You're too expensive."**
- Clarify: "Compared to what?" (other vendors, internal build, doing nothing, the cost of the problem)
- If compared to competitors: Walk through differentiated value, not features. "The price difference is [X]. If [your product] saves [Y hours/month], how does that math work for your team?"
- If general sticker shock: Return to Success Plan. "You told me solving this was worth [their estimated value]. How does our price compare to that?"
- Never discount without getting something in return (longer contract, expanded scope, faster close).

**"Send me a proposal."**
- Clarify: "Before I put something together, can I make sure I'm proposing the right thing? Can we spend 20 minutes to make sure I have your requirements right?"
- A proposal without discovery is a guess. Make sure you've completed discovery before writing anything. (CADTALK proposals: `/ct-proposal`.)

**"We're not ready right now."**
- Clarify: "What would need to happen for the timing to be right?"
- Document the trigger (budget approval, end of current contract, project phase) and schedule follow-up for 2 weeks before that event.
- Move to "Future" stage in CRM (via the sales-crm contract). Do not abandon.

**"I need to get buy-in from [other stakeholder]."**
- Respond: "That's a great step. Can we set up a 30-minute call to introduce me to [them] and walk through this together?"
- Do not hand off to an internal champion and hope they sell for you. Your champion does not know how to sell your product. (Multi-thread — see `references/deal-coach.md` Section 4.)

## Section 7: Weekly Sales Meeting Format

A well-run weekly sales meeting accelerates learning across the team. A poorly run one wastes an hour nobody gets back.

**Recommended Structure (60 minutes):**

**Opening (5 min):** One win from the past week — a deal closed, a great discovery call, a creative solution to a stuck deal.

**Pipeline Review (20 min):** Focus on the 5 most advanced deals in the pipeline (`/ct-report` for the live view). For each:
- What stage is it in?
- What is the next concrete step and who owns it?
- What is the blocker, if any?
- Is the close date realistic? (`/ct-commit` for the honest answer.)

Manager asks, rep answers. This is not a status report — it is a coaching session. If the rep does not know the answer to any of the above, that is the coaching moment.

**Skill Practice (20 min):** One scenario relevant to what the team is facing right now. Examples:
- "Three of us had deals go dark this week. Let's role-play reigniting a dark deal."
- "We lost a deal to [Competitor] yesterday. Let's work through how to handle that objection."
- "We have a new enterprise prospect coming in Friday. Let's practice the executive discovery call."

**Best Practice Share (10 min):** One rep shares something that worked this week — a subject line, a discovery question, a way to handle a specific objection.

**Admin / Announcements (5 min):** Everything operational goes here. Do not let admin eat the meeting.

**Rules:**
- Start on time. End on time.
- Phones off during skill practice.
- Manager contributes observations alongside everyone else — not above them.
- Rotate who runs the best practice share.

## Section 8: New Hire Onboarding Checklist

A rep who fails to ramp is almost always a failure of onboarding, not a failure of talent. Use this checklist for every new hire. **CADTALK-specific ramp: `/ct-train` walks a new rep through the live skill workflow on a mock deal in ~20 minutes — use it in Week 1.**

**Week 1: Orientation and Foundation**
- [ ] Product walk-through with engineering or product (the rep can explain what the product does and why customers buy it)
- [ ] Run `/ct-train` end to end (the mock-deal workflow walkthrough)
- [ ] Shadow 3 discovery calls and 2 demos with senior reps
- [ ] Read the full sales playbook and complete a written comprehension quiz
- [ ] Learn the CRM: how to log activity, update stages, create opportunities (`/ct-crm`)
- [ ] Study the ICP document — can they describe the ideal customer without looking at it? (`/ct-icp`)
- [ ] Complete the competitive battlecard review (`/ct-competitors`)

**Week 2: Guided Practice**
- [ ] Role-play: Opening a cold referral call (pass/fail certification with manager)
- [ ] Role-play: Running a 20-minute discovery call (pass/fail certification)
- [ ] Shadow 3 more discovery calls; take notes on questions asked and responses given
- [ ] Write their own version of the referral email sequence; manager reviews and approves (`/ct-outreach` + `/ct-voice`)

**Week 3–4: Supervised Execution**
- [ ] Run first solo discovery call with manager on the line (debrief immediately after — see `references/deal-coach.md` Section 6)
- [ ] Send first prospecting email campaign; manager reviews response handling
- [ ] Generate first scoping call from outbound or inbound source
- [ ] Weekly 1:1 coaching session focused on specific gaps identified in weeks 1–2

**Weeks 5–8: Independent Ramp**
- [ ] Running solo discovery calls and demos
- [ ] First qualified opportunity created
- [ ] Weekly pipeline reviews with manager
- [ ] Attending weekly team training

**Failure signal at end of Week 2:** Cannot pass discovery call role-play certification. Treat this as a hiring assessment signal, not a training gap.

## Section 9: Enablement Audit Checklist

Use this to assess the current state of sales enablement. Mark each as ✅ exists and works, ⚠️ exists but needs improvement, or ❌ missing.

- [ ] Sales playbook is documented and current (updated in last 6 months)
- [ ] Every rep can explain the ICP without looking at a document
- [ ] Prospecting email templates are standardized and have been A/B tested
- [ ] Qualification framework is documented and consistently applied
- [ ] Demo follows a standard structure (problem-led, not feature-led)
- [ ] Objection handling guide covers the 6–8 most common objections
- [ ] Competitive battlecards exist for top 2–3 competitors
- [ ] New hire onboarding includes role-play certifications
- [ ] Weekly team training session runs consistently every week
- [ ] Manager is conducting weekly 1:1 pipeline coaching sessions
- [ ] Call recording or shadowing is used for active coaching

**Priority order for gaps:** Fix the ICP document and qualification framework first. Without these, everything built on top of them is misaligned.
